  <Slide title="Lesson 14 — How to Analyze Your Games">
    Improvement accelerates when students review games with a consistent method.

    Goals:

    - Build a simple self-analysis routine
    - Identify turning points and recurring mistakes
    - Turn lessons from one game into goals for the next game
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Class plan (30 minutes)">
    - **5 min**: Recap strategy and structure
    - **12 min**: Analysis workflow
    - **10 min**: Puzzle lab (**teacher adds manually**)
    - **3 min**: Individual improvement target
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Beginner game-review workflow">
    1. Replay the full game without an engine.
    2. Mark three turning points (opening, middle game, endgame).
    3. Write what you were calculating and what you missed.
    4. Only then check with engine suggestions.
    5. Set one focus goal for next time.
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="What to look for first">
    - Missed checks, captures, and threats
    - Early king-safety mistakes
    - Piece activity problems
    - Endgame conversion errors

  <Slide title="Puzzle lab + homework setup">
    **Puzzle Lab (10 min):** Teacher inserts manual positions from student games:

    - "What was the best move here?"
    - "Where did the plan break down?"
    - "How could this endgame be converted?"

    **Homework idea:** Analyze one full game with the five-step workflow and
    bring one key lesson to next class.
