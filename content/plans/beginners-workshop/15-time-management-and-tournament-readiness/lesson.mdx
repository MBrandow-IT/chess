  <Slide title="Lesson 15 — Time Management and Tournament Readiness">
    Great moves do not help if they come too late. Students need practical
    habits for clock play and event settings.

    Goals:

    - Use time intentionally in all game phases
    - Build a reliable move routine under pressure
    - Prepare for classroom and tournament game etiquette
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Class plan (30 minutes)">
    - **5 min**: Review analysis takeaways
    - **12 min**: Time-management systems
    - **10 min**: Puzzle lab (**teacher adds manually**)
    - **3 min**: Personal pre-game checklist
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Beginner clock management rules">
    - Spend more time in critical tactical moments
    - Play known opening principles quickly
    - Keep at least a small reserve for the endgame
    - Avoid panic: use checks, captures, and threats to organize thinking
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Move routine under pressure">
    A proven beginner routine:

    1. Opponent threat check
    2. Candidate moves (usually 2–3)
    3. Safety blunder check
    4. Commit and move confidently
