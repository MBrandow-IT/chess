  <Slide title="Lesson 3 — Opening Principles and Fast Development">
    Strong beginners follow principles instead of memorizing long move lists.

    Goals for today:

    - Fight for the center with pawns and pieces
    - Develop minor pieces quickly
    - Castle early and connect rooks
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Class plan (30 minutes)">
    - **5 min**: Review checkmate and special rules
    - **12 min**: Opening principles with examples
    - **10 min**: Puzzle lab (**teacher adds manually**)
    - **3 min**: Exit ticket ("What makes an opening move good?")
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Opening checklist for beginners">
    In your first 8–10 moves, aim to:

    - Control **e4, d4, e5, d5**
    - Develop knights and bishops once each
    - Castle before launching attacks
    - Avoid moving the same piece repeatedly without a reason
    - Avoid early queen adventures

    <ChessBoard fen="r1bqkbnr/pppp1ppp/2n5/4p3/3PP3/5N2/PPP2PPP/RNBQKB1R b KQkq - 2 3" />
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Common opening mistakes">
    - Moving too many pawns and falling behind in development
    - Bringing out the queen too soon
    - Ignoring king safety
    - Chasing one piece while the opponent develops everything

    A simple plan beats random moves almost every time.
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Puzzle lab + homework setup">
    **Puzzle Lab (10 min):** Teacher inserts manual positions for:

    - Best developing move
    - Spot the move that violates opening principles
    - Safe castling opportunities

    **Homework idea:** Play two short games and score your first 10 moves with
    the opening checklist.
  </Slide>
