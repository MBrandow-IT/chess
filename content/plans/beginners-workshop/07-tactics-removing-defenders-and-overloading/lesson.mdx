  <Slide title="Lesson 4 — Tactics II: Removing Defenders, Overloading, and Finishing Attacks">
    Strong tactics often start by identifying one piece that is doing too much.

    Goals:

    - Find key defenders in a position
    - Use exchanges to remove that defender
    - Recognize overloaded pieces and convert into mating attacks
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Class plan (30 minutes)">
    - **5 min**: Review line tactics
    - **12 min**: Defender-focused tactical thinking
    - **10 min**: Puzzle lab (**teacher adds manually**)
    - **3 min**: Debrief and concept check
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Removing the defender">
    If one piece protects a critical square or piece, eliminate it first.

    Typical sequence:

    1. Identify the defender.
    2. Force it away or trade it off.
    3. Win material or deliver mate on the newly weak target.

    <ChessBoard fen="4k3/8/8/2q5/3b4/4N3/4Q3/4K3 w - - 0 1" />
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Overloading and practical play">
    An overloaded piece has multiple defensive jobs at once and cannot maintain
    all of them after a forcing move.

    This concept appears constantly in beginner and intermediate games.
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Puzzle lab + homework setup">
    **Puzzle Lab (10 min):** Teacher inserts manual positions for:

    - Remove-the-defender wins
    - Overloaded queen or rook examples
    - Defensive resources that avoid collapse

    **Homework idea:** Annotate one game position where one defending piece was
    carrying too many responsibilities.
  </Slide>
