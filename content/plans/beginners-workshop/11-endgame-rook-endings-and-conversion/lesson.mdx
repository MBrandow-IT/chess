  <Slide title="Lesson 11 — Rook Endings and Conversion">
    Rook endgames appear often in real beginner games.

    Goals:

    - Place rooks actively behind passed pawns
    - Use checks from behind or the side
    - Convert extra pawns without allowing counterplay
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Class plan (30 minutes)">
    - **5 min**: Opposition recap
    - **12 min**: Core rook-ending principles
    - **10 min**: Puzzle lab (**teacher adds manually**)
    - **3 min**: Practical conversion checklist
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Rook-ending principles beginners can use now">
    - Activate king and rook as soon as possible
    - Keep rook behind passed pawns (yours or your opponent's)
    - Avoid passive rook defense if active checks are available
    - Trade pawns only when it simplifies into a known win

  <Slide title="Converting winning positions">
    Converting means turning an advantage into a point by:

    - Improving piece activity
    - Limiting opponent counterplay
    - Choosing clear plans over flashy tactics

    This mindset wins more games than memorization.
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Puzzle lab + homework setup">
    **Puzzle Lab (10 min):** Teacher inserts manual positions for:

    - Best rook placement behind passed pawns
    - Defensive checking technique
    - Simplify-or-not decisions in winning endgames

    **Homework idea:** Play one rook endgame from a preset position and record
    your plan in three moves.
