  <Slide title="Lesson 7 — Pawn Structure, Smart Trades, and Game Review">
    Pawn structure shapes the middle game and endgame plans.

    Goals:

    - Identify isolated, doubled, and backward pawns
    - Decide when to trade pieces based on structure
    - Build a short post-game review habit after each game
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Class plan (30 minutes)">
    - **5 min**: Piece activity recap
    - **12 min**: Pawn-structure patterns
    - **10 min**: Puzzle lab (**teacher adds manually**)
    - **3 min**: Trade-decision reflection
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Common beginner structures">
    - **Isolated pawn**: can be a weakness, but gives open lines
    - **Doubled pawns**: weak in endgames, can control key squares
    - **Pawn majorities**: can create passed pawns later

    Strong players choose plans that match these structures.

    <ChessBoard fen="r1bq1rk1/pp3ppp/2n1pn2/2pp4/3P4/2P1PN2/PP1N1PPP/R1BQ1RK1 w - - 0 1" />
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Smart trade rules for beginners">
    - Trade when it improves your structure or king safety
    - Avoid automatic trades that activate the opponent's pieces
    - If ahead in material, simplify carefully
    - If behind, keep pieces and seek activity
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Puzzle lab + homework setup">
    **Puzzle Lab (10 min):** Teacher inserts manual positions for:

    - Best trade or no-trade decisions
    - Choosing plans from pawn structure
    - Finding outpost squares for knights

    **Homework idea:** Review one game and label each major trade as "helpful,"
    "neutral," or "harmful" based on resulting structure.
  </Slide>
