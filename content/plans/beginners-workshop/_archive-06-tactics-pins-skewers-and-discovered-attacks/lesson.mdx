  <Slide title="Lesson 6 — Pins, Skewers, and Discovered Attacks">
    Line tactics happen when pieces share files, ranks, or diagonals.

    Goals:

    - Tell the difference between pins and skewers
    - Understand discovered attacks and discovered checks
    - Improve tactical scanning before each move
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Class plan (30 minutes)">
    - **5 min**: Fork review
    - **12 min**: Line tactics explained with board examples
    - **10 min**: Puzzle lab (**teacher adds manually**)
    - **3 min**: Reflection ("Which tactic feels easiest to spot?")
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Pins and skewers in one view">
    - **Pin**: front piece cannot move because a more valuable piece is behind.
    - **Skewer**: front piece is forced away, exposing a less valuable piece.

    Think: pin = "stuck in place," skewer = "forced to move."

    <ChessBoard fen="4k3/8/8/2b5/3N4/8/4Q3/4K3 b - - 0 1" />
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Discovered attacks">
    A discovered attack happens when one piece moves and opens a line for
    another piece behind it.

    If the revealed line gives check, that is a **discovered check**, one of
    the strongest tactical ideas in chess.
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Puzzle lab + homework setup">
    **Puzzle Lab (10 min):** Teacher inserts manual positions for:

    - Absolute and relative pins
    - Skewer opportunities
    - Discovered attack combinations

    **Homework idea:** Review one game and tag each tactic as fork, pin,
    skewer, or discovered attack.
  </Slide>
