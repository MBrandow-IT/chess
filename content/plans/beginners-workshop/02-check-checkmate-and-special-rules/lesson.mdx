  <Slide title="Check and checkmate fundamentals">
    A king in check is under attack and must respond immediately.

    There are only three legal responses:

    1. Move the king away.
    2. Capture the attacking piece.
    3. Block the attack (if the attacker is a sliding piece).

    If none of those are possible, it is **checkmate** and the game is over.

    <ChessBoard fen="4k3/8/8/8/8/8/4r3/4K3 w - - 0 1" />
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="The three special rules beginners need early">
    - **Castling**: King moves two squares toward a rook, rook jumps over.
      Great for king safety and rook activity.
    - **En passant**: A special pawn capture right after an opponent pawn moves
      two squares and lands next to your pawn.
    - **Promotion**: A pawn that reaches the last rank becomes a queen, rook,
      bishop, or knight (usually a queen).
  </Slide>
