  <Slide title="Lesson 4 — Opening Traps to Avoid">
    Most beginner losses happen before move 12 because of one-move blunders.

    Today students will:

    - Recognize "free piece" traps
    - Spot early mating threats (especially on f2/f7)
    - Use a simple blunder-check routine before moving
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Class plan (30 minutes)">
    - **5 min**: Review opening principles
    - **12 min**: Trap patterns and safety checks
    - **10 min**: Puzzle lab (**teacher adds manually**)
    - **3 min**: Personal anti-blunder checklist
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="What traps usually target">
    - Weak king squares (f2 for White, f7 for Black)
    - Unprotected pieces
    - Loose back-rank coordination
    - Greedy captures that ignore threats

    <ChessBoard fen="r1bqkb1r/pppp1ppp/2n2n2/4p3/3PP1Q1/5N2/PPP2PPP/RNB1KB1R b KQkq - 2 4" />
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Three questions before every move">
    1. What is my opponent threatening right now?
    2. Is my move safe, or does it drop material/checkmate?
    3. Does my move improve king safety or piece activity?

    This routine prevents most beginner traps immediately.
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Puzzle lab + homework setup">
    **Puzzle Lab (10 min):** Teacher inserts manual positions for:

    - Spotting one-move tactics against f2/f7
    - Choosing safe alternatives to greedy captures
    - Defending against early queen attacks

    **Homework idea:** Play one game and annotate three moments where you used
    the three-question check.
  </Slide>
