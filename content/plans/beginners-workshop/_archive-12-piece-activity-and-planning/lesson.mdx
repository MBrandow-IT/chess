  <Slide title="Lesson 12 — Piece Activity and Planning">
    Beginner progress depends on making useful plans, not random moves.

    Goals:

    - Evaluate piece activity quickly
    - Improve your worst-placed piece first
    - Build plans around targets and open lines
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Class plan (30 minutes)">
    - **5 min**: Endgame conversion review
    - **12 min**: Planning framework
    - **10 min**: Puzzle lab (**teacher adds manually**)
    - **3 min**: Plan-writing exercise
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Simple planning framework">
    Ask:

    1. Who has the safer king?
    2. Which pieces are active or passive?
    3. What weak squares, pawns, or files can I target?

    Then choose one concrete plan for the next 3–4 moves.

  <Slide title="Improve your worst piece">
    This rule is used across many successful beginner curricula:

    - If no tactic exists, improve your least active piece.
    - Coordinate pieces before launching attacks.
    - Avoid creating weaknesses without a clear gain.
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Puzzle lab + homework setup">
    **Puzzle Lab (10 min):** Teacher inserts manual positions for:

    - Best improving move
    - Plan selection between two candidate ideas
    - Targeting weak pawns or weak squares

    **Homework idea:** After each move in one game, state your plan in a short
    phrase ("improve bishop," "contest open file," "trade into better ending").
  </Slide>
