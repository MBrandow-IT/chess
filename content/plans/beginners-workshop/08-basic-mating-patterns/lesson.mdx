    Midway through the workshop, students now convert advantages into wins.

    Goals:

    - **12 min**: Mating pattern instruction
    - **10 min**: Puzzle lab (**teacher adds manually**)
    - **3 min**: Student verbal explanation of one pattern
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Back-rank mate pattern">
    Back-rank mates happen when the king is trapped by its own pawns and has no
    escape squares.

    Attackers often use a rook or queen on the back rank to finish.

    <ChessBoard fen="6k1/5ppp/8/8/8/8/5PPP/5RK1 w - - 0 1" />
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Ladder mate and king restriction">
    In ladder mate, two heavy pieces repeatedly cut off ranks or files while
    pushing the enemy king to the edge.

    Rule of thumb: first limit the king, then deliver checkmate.
  </Slide>

  <Slide title="Puzzle lab + homework setup">
    **Puzzle Lab (10 min):** Teacher inserts manual positions for:

    - Back-rank mate in one or two
    - Ladder mate technique
    - Identifying missing escape squares

    **Homework idea:** Play from a winning position and focus on forcing
    checkmate efficiently rather than grabbing extra pawns.
  </Slide>
